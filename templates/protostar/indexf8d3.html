Call Centre

Western Central RSC operates a 24 hours call centre to receive complaints from consumers from any part of the country. This centre is operated throughout the year and is multy lingual.  Consumers can use toll free number 1939 from any land phone or mobile phone to make any complaint. Complaints received are immediately informed to respective Area Engineer's or Officers in Charge for prompt actions. Within the Western Central RSC stand by crews are available in day and night to attend such complaints.

Non Revenue Water

In any water supply system, water that produced and released to the distribution system does not fully generate revenue for the Water Authority. This is mainly due to the loss of water through the distribution system. The quantity of water that does not produces any revenue is usually termed as Non Revenue Water (NRW). This is usually expressed as a percentage of water supplied to the system. While NRW is a threat to the financial sustainability of Water Authority, it is commonly used as a key performance indicator in a Water Authority.

The average NRW figure for Western Central RSc is about 36% during the year 2014. While Towns East of Colombo (North an South) have comparative low NRW figures around 18%, Colombo City records a substantially high value for NRW around 47%. This is mainly due to the large number of water leaks occurring daily in the distribution system due to the aging of pipes. The other contributory factors for NRW are free water supplied to the people living in tenement gardens, theft of water due to illegal connections and bypasses and losses due to administrative problems including defective meters, not taking meter readings, unmetered premises etc.

Following major steps have been taken during the last few years to reduce NRW,

  • Strengthening of attending pipe leaks.
  • Replacement of corroded pipes.
  • Provision of individual connections in tenement gardens and reducing public stand posts.
  • Strengthening of raiding and prosecution of illegal water users.
  • Replacement of defective water meters.
  • Improving in metering and billing process to reduce NRW.

Unauthorized Consumption of Water Reduction

All citizens of the country have the right to request for an individual drinking water supply. National Water Supply & Drainage Board (NWSDB) evaluates the request based on the availability of distribution system, availability of water system pressure etc. To install the water connection and send the bill, address proof is needed. It could be in the form of assessment tax or number assigned by the local authority.

 A legal water supply would have a connection provided by NWSDB, quantity of water consumed will be measured by a meter, having NWSDB marked on it. Property of the NWSDB ends with the meter. Only authorized persons could tamper with NWSDB property, in the event unauthorized person tampers with NWSDB property it is a punishable offence. It is the responsibility of the water user to ensure that all the consumption are quantified by the meter.

Employees of the NWSDB have the authority to enter private premises to check legality of consumption of water. NWSDB has a separate section specialist in detection of unauthorized consumption. On average 15,000 premises are checked and over 1,500 detection are made.

Reduction of Free Water

There are free water common outlets in the distribution system to serve the population who do not have legal water supply. These outlets have been installed when the water was supplied free by the local authority. The current trend to have a green environment by reduction of wastage is managed by measuring the quantity of water consumed and sending monthly bill. To encourage persons to obtain individual connections a program called “Randya” was initiated in 2000.

Randya Program

Randya program was initiated in year 2000 by providing individual connections to the underserved settlement with individual water connection after disconnecting of common out lets. The residence had to pay affordable charge of Rs 4000/+ plus VAT for the connections. This was further eased by payment being made in installments consisting of down payment of Rs 250/= + VAT ( Rs 480 /= ) as the first installment and balance in 37 equal installments of Rs 100/= . The community was expected contribute by excavation of trenches for pipe laying.

Formation of Customer Society

Some residence in the underserved settlements do not have sufficient space to accommodate water supply or the required drainage facility. Opportunity was extended them to make a group of residence to identify them as user of the free water outlet and be made responsible for maintenance and settlement of water bill. The group had a volunteer to whom the monthly bill would be sent and be responsible to settle the bills. Metering of Common outlet and requesting common outlet users to pay for water consumed was initiated in 2010 this process was accelerated lately. A special low unit rate of Rs 3/= for each m3 was introduced with intention to sustain the initiative. Nearly 1700 societies have been formed and the water consumed is metered.
